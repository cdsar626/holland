Project Summary

I joined Melvin Jewelry in February 2014 as their first teen intern. In this position, I wore many hats from emailing manufacturers, to packaging and shipping jewelry, to operating trunk shows, researching trends, etc. I learned ample skills in the fields of design thinking, marketing, and entrepreneurship.

Challenge

The Melvin Jewelry looked too "old" for younger girls

Solution

"Mini Melvin" - a trendy jewelry line for girls ages 7-14

Step 1: Observe

My sister (right) and her friends wearing Melvin necklaces

My younger sister and her friends loved the sample jewelry I brought home from Melvin. A lot of the jewelry, however, looked too old or large on them. I pitched the idea of starting a children's line. The Melvin team loved the idea and gave me the responsibility of spearheading the line.

Step 2: Design

Before I picked up my pen and my sketchpad, I asked my sister and her friends what they like about Melvin jewelry. Many of them showed interest in the pom and tassel collections. A lot of them also talked about how they love the color combinations. With that in mind, I began sketching.

Step 3: Build

I got feedback on my initial designs from my sister and the head designer and started to make jewelry. As I chose which colors to use, I referenced the colors in the necklaces that my sister and her friends wore the most.

Step 4: Test and Iterate

Once I put together some pieces, I brought them and got more feedback from my sister. I did this multiple times before bringing the products tomarket.

Step 5: Bring to Market

When I had all of the sample pieces of the collection, it was time to bring it to market. The marketing coordinator on the team helped me create an instagram for the line. I got the inspiration for the name of the line "Mini Melvin" from my little sister whom I refer to as my mini-me.
